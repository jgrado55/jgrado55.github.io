Let us take a look at the Zapata Suite.

A vibrant, maximalist tribute to Emiliano Zapata. Walls are covered salon-style with vintage portraits and revolutionary imagery of the hero in a sombrero and bandoliers. The bed explodes with a hand-embroidered Oaxacan quilt in bold colors and pillows printed with Zapata’s face and slogans.

Dark wooden furniture, antique lanterns, charro touches, and Zapotec rugs complete the early-20th-century Mexican ranchero feel. Intense, colorful, and unapologetically revolutionary — This room puts you to sleep under the watchful gaze of a legend.

The Zapata Suite – ¡Tierra y Libertad! luxury

Bedroom

  • Vibrant hand-embroidered Mexican quilt with bold floral patterns in red, yellow, green, and black
  • Bed piled high with throw pillows featuring Emiliano Zapata’s face and name printed on them
  • Walls completely covered in a dense salon-style hanging of framed black-and-white and sepia photographs and illustrations of Zapata (some on horseback, some with revolutionary troops)
  • Antique dark-wood dresser topped with old-fashioned oil lanterns
  • Colorful woven rugs layered over rustic wooden flooring
  • Overall atmosphere of a cozy folk-revolutionary shrine

Bathroom

  • Warm amber lighting from wall sconces flanking a large mirror
  • Walls saturated with bold red-and-black revolutionary posters shouting “¡Tierra y Libertad!” and “¡Viva Zapata!”
  • Multiple large, ornate gold-framed oil portraits of Emiliano Zapata staring directly at the viewer
  • Vintage double-sink vanity with marble countertop and distressed cream cabinetry
  • Rustic dark-wood flooring and sheer white curtains softening the window
  • Dramatic, almost theatrical Mexican Revolution gallery feel

Study

  • Large central painting of a volcano (likely Popocatépetl) under a dramatic sky
  • Surrounding walls packed with framed portraits of Emiliano Zapata and revolutionary posters declaring “¡Tierra y Libertad!” and “¡Viva México!”
  • Antique rifle mounted horizontally above a heavy wooden desk
  • Leather-bound books stacked on shelves and desk, mixed with traditional pottery and oil lamps
  • Dark wood furniture, lace tablecloth, patterned curtains, and woven rugs
  • Dim, scholarly atmosphere like the private retreat of a dedicated Zapatista intellectual
